Software Security & Assurance — Executive Summary (2025)

Global view with India specifics • Past year focus • Ready for HTML publishing

DevSecOps Supply-Chain Zero Trust SBOM AI & PQC

Executive Snapshot

  • Exploits and vulnerabilities continue to surge; supply-chain and API weaknesses dominate breach causes.
  • Leaders embed security into SDLC (DevSecOps), automate checks, and enforce rapid fix SLAs.
  • Regulators raising the bar: US attestations, EU Cyber Resilience Act, India DPDP + CERT-In 6-hour reporting.
  • Finance leads in maturity; healthcare and public sector improving but hampered by legacy tech.
  • Emerging shifts: AI-assisted defense/dev, Zero-Trust defaults, SBOM ubiquity, post-quantum crypto transition.

Threats & Exposure

  • Critical CVEs weaponized within days; patch latency is the risk multiplier.
  • Linux/macOS vulns spiked; browsers & office software heavily targeted.
  • Top weaknesses: cryptographic failures, injection flaws, access control gaps.
  • Ransomware & supply-chain compromise remain top impact vectors.

What Works Now

  • Shift-left: SAST, SCA, secrets scanning in CI/CD; DAST on staging; IaC/K8s checks.
  • Risk triage: Fix crypto/injection first; enforce SLAs.
  • Toolchain consolidation: Unified dashboards reduce noise.
  • Secure by design: Threat modeling, least privilege, zero-trust segmentation.
  • Supply-chain controls: SBOM, signed provenance (SLSA), vendor attestation.
  • People & process: Security champions, targeted training, pen-tests, bug bounties.

Assurance Stack

Regulation

  • US: NIST SSDF, SBOM attestations, Zero-Trust mandates.
  • EU: Cyber Resilience Act, NIS2, GDPR.
  • Global: ISO 27001/27034, PCI DSS 4.0, sectoral rules (HIPAA, DORA).
  • India: DPDP Act, CERT-In 6-hr reporting, RBI/SEBI/IRDAI directives, NCIIPC guidance.

Sector Highlights

Finance: Fast remediation, high maturity.

Healthcare: Ransomware risk, legacy devices, improving patching.

Public Sector: Zero-Trust rollouts, supply-chain audits, legacy modernization.

Emerging Tech

  • AI in AppSec: triage, code review, autonomous fixes.
  • Post-Quantum Crypto: NIST PQC standards, pilot adoption.
  • Hardware defenses: CET/MTE, confidential computing enclaves.
  • Supply-chain integrity: Sigstore signing, SLSA provenance.
  • Passwordless auth: FIDO2/Passkeys adoption rising.

90-Day Action Plan

  1. Inventory apps/APIs; generate SBOMs.
  2. Enable CI/CD guardrails (SAST, SCA, secrets, IaC scans).
  3. Remediate top crypto/injection flaws; add virtual patches.
  4. Run threat modeling for crown-jewel services.
  5. Commission a focused pen-test.
  6. Adopt a secure-dev policy aligned to NIST/ISO.
  7. Align to India’s DPDP & CERT-In requirements.
